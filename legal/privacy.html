Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy explains what information may be collected when you use Network Packet Dictionary, how it is used, and the choices you have. This site is a static reference website.

Some pages may display advertising. You can also control cookies in your browser settings.

Information we collect

We do not ask you to create an account. However, when you visit this website, limited technical information may be processed automatically by standard web infrastructure and third-party services, such as:

  • Device and browser information (e.g., user agent, language, screen size)
  • Approximate location derived from IP address (city/region level)
  • Basic usage events (pages viewed, referrers, timestamps)

We do not intentionally collect sensitive personal data. Please avoid sending sensitive data in any messages you send to us.

Advertising (Google AdSense)

Some pages may show ads served by third-party vendors, including Google. These vendors may use cookies or similar technologies to serve ads, measure performance, and limit repeated ads.

Google may use advertising cookies to help serve ads based on a user’s prior visits to this website and/or other websites. You can learn about controlling ad personalization through Google’s ad settings, and you can also control cookies in your browser.

Cookies and similar technologies

Cookies may be used for advertising, security, and basic measurement of site usage. You can disable or delete cookies in your browser settings. Note that some site features may not work as intended if cookies are blocked.

Local processing

When this site provides tools, they are designed to run in your browser. We do not require you to upload packet captures or files to use the site’s reference content.

External links

This site may link to external websites (for example, vendor documentation). We are not responsible for their content or privacy practices. Please review the privacy policies of any third-party sites you visit.

Data retention

We do not maintain user accounts. Logs and aggregated metrics, if enabled through infrastructure or analytics, are retained only as long as needed for security, debugging, and site improvement.

Your choices

  • You can control cookies through your browser settings.
  • You can use privacy features such as blocking third-party cookies or using private browsing modes.

Contact

If you have questions about this policy, please use the contact page.

Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Changes will be posted on this page.
